Our Story

Discover the passion behind The Artists' Roast

From Dream to Reality

The Artists' Roast was born in 2019 from a simple dream: to create a space where exceptional coffee meets creative expression. Founded by Sarah Johnson, a former graphic designer turned passionate barista, our coffee shop emerged from the vibrant arts district of Brooklyn with a mission to serve both the local community and visiting artists.

What started as a small 20-seat café has grown into a beloved neighborhood hub, but we've never lost sight of our core values. Every morning, we hand-select our beans from fair-trade farms, supporting sustainable practices and the communities that grow our coffee. Our partnership with local bakeries ensures fresh pastries daily, while our walls showcase rotating exhibitions from emerging artists.

Our Mission

We believe that great coffee has the power to fuel creativity and build community. Our mission extends beyond serving exceptional beverages – we're committed to creating an inclusive environment where artists, entrepreneurs, students, and neighbors can gather, collaborate, and find inspiration.

Every cup we serve supports fair-trade practices, and every event we host strengthens the creative fabric of our neighborhood. We're not just a coffee shop; we're a catalyst for artistic expression and meaningful connections.

Community First

The heart of The Artists' Roast beats with community spirit. Our weekly open mic nights have launched local musicians' careers, while our monthly art exhibitions provide emerging artists with their first gallery experience. We offer discounted workspace memberships to freelance creatives and host quarterly networking events that bring together Brooklyn's diverse artistic community.

From sourcing locally-made ceramics for our mugs to partnering with neighborhood organizations for charity drives, we're deeply rooted in Brooklyn's creative ecosystem. When you visit us, you're not just a customer – you're part of our extended family of dreamers, makers, and coffee enthusiasts.
